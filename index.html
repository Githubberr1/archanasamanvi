Musical Legacy

Based in the cultural hub of Udupi, the Lathangi Sisters embody a rare synthesis of traditional Gurukula values and modern scholarly research.

The Lineage of Lathangi

Their journey began in 2008 at the renowned "Lathangi" house in Udupi, under the tutelage of Vid. Vasanthalakshmi Hebbar and Dr. V. Aravinda Hebbar. This early immersion in a musical household provided them with a foundation in the classic Gurukula vasa tradition.


A transformative milestone occurred in 2015 when they were accepted as disciples by the legendary maestro Chitravina N. Ravikiran. Under his scholarly guidance, their music has evolved to include complex rhythmic structures and profound raga explorations, making them a unique duo in the contemporary Carnatic landscape.

Detailed Biography

While not filial sisters, their synchronized artistry and shared academic discipline have created a powerful musical identity.

MSc Physics AIR "A" Grade

Archana Upadhyaya

Archana's musical journey is a testament to the balance between scientific discipline and artistic soul. As an MSc graduate in Physics from Madras University, she brings a methodical depth to her performances. Her voice is characterized by its stability, resonant depth, and the ability to navigate the intricacies of traditional Carnatic ragas with meditative ease.

Recognized as an "A" graded artist of All India Radio, Archana has been a pivotal force in the duo’s rise. She has performed extensively at prestigious venues across India and was a key part of their successful maiden US tour in 2025, performing at the Cleveland Thyagaraja Festival. Her dedication to the Haridasa tradition is reflected in her significant contributions to the Daasaarchane recording project.

MA Music AIR "A" Grade

Vidushi P. Samanvi

Samanvi is widely regarded as one of the most promising vocalists of her generation. Currently pursuing her MA in Music at Madras University, she possesses a nimble, expressive voice capable of remarkable rhythmic precision. In 2022, she was honored with the prestigious "MS Subbulakshmi Voice of the Year" award by The Hindu, a recognition that solidified her standing in the fraternity.

As a direct "A" grade artist of AIR, Samanvi’s technical brilliance and creative manodharma complement the duo’s synchronized sound. She is a recipient of the MS Subbulakshmi Fellowship and the Mani Krishnaswamy Award. Her scholarly curiosity led her to co-author "Keerthanas of Karnataka," showcasing her commitment to documenting and preserving the region's rich musical heritage alongside her performance career.

Repertoire & Projects

Literary Contributions

Co-authored Keerthanas of Karnataka (2018), a scholarly volume documenting rare compositions and their notations.

Daasaarchane

Recorded 101 compositions of various Haridasas, celebrating the spiritual depth of the Dasa Sahitya tradition.

Lecture-Demos

Presenting advanced demonstrations on concepts like Grahabhedhas (Modal shift of tonic) at major music academies.
