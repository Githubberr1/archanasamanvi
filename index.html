Musical Legacy

Lineage & Training

The journey of the Lathangi Sisters began in 2008 at the renowned "Lathangi" house in Udupi, under the mentorship of Vid. Vasanthalakshmi Hebbar and Prof. Aravinda Hebbar. Their skills were further honed by Vid. Ranjani Hebbar.


A defining milestone occurred in 2015 when they were accepted as disciples by the internationally acclaimed maestro Sangeet Samraat Chitravina N Ravikiran. Under his scholarly guidance, they have expanded their repertoire to include advanced rhythmic concepts and rare compositions.

The Artistes

Archana Upadhyaya and Samanvi, popularly known as the Lathangi Sisters, are celebrated Carnatic vocalists whose artistry blends youthful dynamism with scholarly depth. Though not filial sisters, their musical bond and synchrony have earned them a distinct identity in the Carnatic music fraternity.


Both artistes share a strong academic background alongside their musical excellence. Archana is an MSc graduate from Madras University, while Samanvi is currently pursuing her MA in Music at Madras University. Their journey is marked by prestigious recognitions, including being honored as the "MS Subbulakshmi Voice of the Year" by The Hindu in 2022. Together, they represent a harmonious blend of traditional Gurukula values and modern scholarly research.
