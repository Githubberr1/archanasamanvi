Musical Legacy

The Lineage of Lathangi

The journey of the Lathangi Sisters began in 2008 at the renowned "Lathangi" house in Udupi, under the mentorship of Vid. Vasanthalakshmi Hebbar and Dr. V. Aravinda Hebbar. This early immersion in the traditional Gurukula vasa environment provided the foundational bond for their synchronized artistry[cite: 77, 78].


A defining milestone occurred in 2015 when they were accepted as disciples by the internationally acclaimed maestro Chitravina N. Ravikiran. Under his scholarly guidance, they have expanded their repertoire to include advanced rhythmic concepts, establishing themselves as torchbearers of the tradition[cite: 80, 97].

Detailed Biography

Equal partners in music and scholarship, Archana and Samanvi represent a rare distinction in their generation[cite: 88].

MSc Physics (Madras Univ) AIR "A" Grade

Vidushi Archana Upadhyaya

Archana Upadhyaya's musical style is marked by meditative raga exploration and a voice of remarkable depth[cite: 76]. An MSc graduate in Physics from Madras University, she brings a disciplined, scholarly rigor to the Gurukula tradition she has followed since 2008[cite: 96, 134]. As an "A" graded artist of All India Radio, she is recognized among the finest of her generation[cite: 88, 115].

Archana was a central figure in the duo's 2025 maiden US tour, performing at prestigious stages like the Cleveland Thyagaraja Festival[cite: 85, 101]. Her dedication to preserving the Haridasa tradition is evident in her work with the Daasaarchane project, recording over 100 rare compositions[cite: 92, 126].

MA Music (Madras Univ) AIR "A" Grade

Vidushi P. Samanvi

Samanvi is celebrated for her technical brilliance and expressive manodharma[cite: 76]. Currently an MA student at Madras University, she was honored with the prestigious "MS Subbulakshmi Voice of the Year" award in 2022 by The Hindu[cite: 119, 135]. As a direct "A" grade artist of All India Radio, her performances are noted for their youthful dynamism[cite: 76, 88].

Beyond performance, Samanvi is a dedicated scholar, co-authoring the book Keerthanas of Karnataka in 2018[cite: 92, 124]. Her ability to bridge rigorous stage performance with academic documentation has earned her awards like the MS Subbulakshmi Fellowship and the Mani Krishnaswamy Award[cite: 89, 111].

Repertoire & Projects
